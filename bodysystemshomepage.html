Skeletal System



The skeletal system is what gives the body structure. It protects our internal organs.It also stores minerals in the bone marrow. The skeletal muscles attach to the bones and allow movement. It includes the bones, the ligaments, the the joints and the tendons. There are three types of joints: the hinge joint(in your knees), the pivot joint(in your elbows), the gliding joint(in your wrist), and the ball and socket joint (in your hip)


Digestive System



The digestive system breaks down food and extracts the minerals and nutrients.Digestion starts in the mouth where it is broken down by saliva. The it is sent down your esophagus and into the stomach, where it is further broken down by digestive juices(which are made in the pancreas). Then the fat is broken down It includes the esophagus, the mouth(the teeth preform mechanical digestion), the stomach(which preforms much of the chemical digestion), the intestines(the small intestine extracts nutrients, the large intestine removes excess water and prepares feces), the gallbladder(which helps break down fat), the pancreas(which secretes digestive juices), the liver(which processes the nutrients extractes in the small intestine) and the anus(which removes the feces from the body).


Circulatory System


The circulatory system moves materials around the body(such as oxygen and nutrients). It involves the heart(which pumps the blood), the viens(which are like highways for the blood), the capillaries(which takes the nutrients out of the blood and puts it in the surrounding tissue)and the arteries(which moves the blood from the heart to the rest of the body).


Respiratory System



The respiratory system supplies the body with oxygen. It involves the nose and mouth(which suck the oxygen in), the trachea(which moves the air to the lungs), and the lungs(which process the air and put it in the blood stream).

Excretory System

The excretory system removes liquid and gaseous waste from the body. It involves the lungs(which remove carbon dioxide from the body), the kidneys(which act as a filter) the ureter(which moves the urine to the urinary bladder), the urinary bladder(which stores the urine) and the urethra(which removes the urine from the body).


Nervous System


The nervous system controls every system in your body(even the involuntary ones). It also includes a part of the brain called the prefrontal cortex which allows deep thinking and seperates us from animals. The nervous system includes the six senses(which gather data of surroundings), the brain(which processes information), the nerve net(which sends signals to the brain from all over the body) and the spinal cord(a pathway for messages sent to the brain).


Muscular System



The muscular systems main job is to mainly just help many of the other systems in the body. It includes every muscle in the human body.


Integumentary system



The integumentary system protects your body from the outside world. It includes the skin, hair and nails. The skin helps regulate temperatures and protect the body from the outside world.

Immune System


The immune system fights off diseases. It includes the lymphnodes

The integumentary system protects the body from outside threats. It also regulates temperature. It includes the skin, hair and nails.

System interactions


The muscular system helps with the respiratory system(the diaphram expands and contracts the lungs).It also helps with the digestive system(the smooth muscles that move the food along their path) and the skeletal system(the skeletal muscles are attached to the skeleton and contract and relax to allow movement).


The skeletal system helps the digestive system by storing nutrients). It also helps the circulatory system (the bone marrow makes blood).


The respiratory systemhelps the circulatory system(the lungs supply oxygen to the circulatory system)


The nervous system helps every system in your body(it gives them commands)
